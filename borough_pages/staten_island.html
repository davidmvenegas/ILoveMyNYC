Staten Island

Begin by taking the Staten Island Ferry, a world-class service that provides everyone with a free ride to an island getaway filled with historic architecture, gardens, and incredible views of the Statue of Liberty and Lower Manhattan. As the third largest borough in the City, it has over 170 parks that cover nearly a third of the island. SI also played a prominent role during the Revolutionary War. Before the British arrived in NY, George Washington used it to give him advanced warning in what eventually would become the Battle of Long Island. But SI isn’t just rich with history. It also has wildlife refuges, numerous zoos, botanical gardens, miles of beaches, and many family-friendly attractions. With its nature and serenity, this borough has the potential to make you feel as though you have left the city entirely.
