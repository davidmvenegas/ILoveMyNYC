WHY WE NYC

Its opportunity.

With over one million businesses, there isn't an industry that doesn't operate in NYC. If we were a country, the economy would be the tenth-largest in the world, beating out entire countries like Canada and Russia. The saying “if you can make it in NYC, you can make it anywhere” has never been more true. You can come here with a dream, work hard and achieve it.

Its history.

NYC is a universal symbol of freedom and democracy. It played a vital role in America’s independence even serving as the nation's first capital. Abolitionism, Women’s Suffrage and many other Civil Rights movements all originated from NYC. There’s a certain pride in being in the most culturally and politically significant city in the world.

Its architecture.

From One World Trade Center to the Empire State Building, Manhattan’s iconic skyline is renowned all over the world. And with the world’s most subway stations it’s easy to explore the numerous tree-lined cobblestone streets, bustling avenues, historic apartments and tucked away coffee shops. There’s no question that NYC defines the urban lifestyle.

Its spirit.

There's no other city on the planet with an energy that can compete with the city that never sleeps. The fast pace, the buzzing traffic, the hustle and bustle of the people, the 24-hour life, and the creative spirit make NYC one of the most vibrant places in the world. An ordinary day can turn into an extraordinary experience in a New York minute.

Its diversity.

Know as the cultural capital of the world, the city is home to the most foreign born citizens in the entire world and is the most linguistically diverse city on earth with approximately 800 languages spoken. At its heart, NYC is a city of immigrants. No matter who you are or where you come from, NYC can be your home.

Its art.

NYC is rich with world acclaimed, fashion, dance, music, and theater - most notably Broadway - the world's performing capital. Hip Hop and Rap began in Bronx and Jazz was popularized in Harlem. From concerts to art gallerys, NYC has it all. For anyone seeking inspiration and creativity, NYC is the place to be.

G e t t i n g     A r o u n d     N Y C

bike

There are infinite reasons to enjoy New York’s ever-expanding bike network. Growing from 250 miles of bike lanes a decade ago, to nearly 1,200 today, you can now ride your bike pretty much anywhere. Join the nearly 1 million bicyclists who regularly make use of NYC’s growing network of bike-friendly streets, roads and paths. If you don’t want to own your own bike, Citi Bike - New York City's bike sharing system - has thousands of bikes at hundreds of stations, all available 24/7 every day of the year. You can conveniently unlock a bike at any station, ride wherever you want and check in the bike at any other station. Daily, three-day and annual passes are available, making it a great option for visitors and locals alike.

* E-Scooters which offer a similar form of transportation were previously banned in NYC but have just recently been legalized as of Nov 2020

subway

The subway is the most efficient way to get around New York City. If you’re traveling more than a mile, it will get you from any part of the city to another often times faster than a taxi or uber. The subway system never stops. It’s 230 miles of tracks - which if laid end to end would stretch from NYC to Chicago - run trains 24 hours a day, seven days a week, 365 days a year. OMNY, which stands for One Metro New York, is currently rolling out and will eventually replace the plastic swipeable MetroCard with contactless tap cards. Currently, the MetroCard provides you with the option to reload, or purchase 7 day and 30 day unlimited passes.

ferry

The ferry is a lovely way of getting around NYC. The world-famous Staten Island Ferry (featured in Marvel's Spiderman: Homecoming) is completely free of charge, running a 20 min route between Staten Island and downtown Manhattan 24/7 every half hour. Another fleet of smaller, faster ferry's are operated by Hornblower and provides New Yorkers with a dependable way to commute and connect in Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens and the Bronx along the East River. Their state-of-the-art vessels offer comfortable conveniences including charging stations, WiFi, temperature controlled cabins, and onboard refreshments.

car

Driving is the least prefered method of transportation in NYC. Public transit in New York, as in most major cities, is far more efficient and far less stressful than driving. Using your car to go 10 blocks for dinner or to the theater will likely take more time than the subway, or even walking. Plus, it will definitely cost much more for parking. But if you need to get somewhere in a hurry, there's always the option of hailing a taxi or using a ridesharing service like Uber or Lyft.

THE BOROUGHS

take a closer look into the five boroughs that make up NYC

Staten Island

Beyond a charming North Shore rich with maritime history, are the greenest borough's lovely beaches and vast parkland.

Brooklyn

You won’t find more stylish restaurants, bars, art galleries, or indie shops than inside Brooklyn's lively, multicultural neighborhoods.

Manhattan

Manhattan truly feels like the heart of it all. Broadway and Times Square are a few of many famous attractions that sit at its electric center.

The Bronx

What do home-run heroes, hip-hop history and hand-pulled mozzarella have in common? You can find them all in the Bronx.

Queens

Queens is one of the most diverse places in the world. It's a major destination for sports fans, nature lovers and modern-art aficionados.
