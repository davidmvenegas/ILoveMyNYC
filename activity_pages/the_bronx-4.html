Van Cortlandt Park

Van Cortlandt Park

As the third-largest park in the City, Van Cortlandt Park boasts the nation's first public golf course. In addition to golf, the park offers tons of green space ideal for picnics, sports fields for the athletic and playgrounds for kids and the young at heart.

Soundview Park

Soundview Park

Called the "Gateway to the Bronx River," this park is situated where the Bronx River opens into the East River. When the city began renovation on the original 93 acres of land nearly a decade ago, the entire area was composed of marshland. Today it has bike baths, waterside benches, baseball diamonds, kayak/canoe launches and more.

Crotona Park

Crotona Park

Not only does this park boast the Bronx's largest swimming pool, it also has a 3.3-acre lake, 20 tennis courts and over a dozen different playgrounds. The 127.5-acre space also includes courts for all kinds of sports, dog runs, beautiful wildlife and designated areas to fire up the grill.
