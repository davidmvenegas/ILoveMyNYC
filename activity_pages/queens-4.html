Jamaica Bay Wildlife Refuge

Jamaica Bay Wildlife Refuge

For bird watchers, this is the place to be. With more than 325 species of birds, a salt marsh, woods and freshwater ponds - this wildlife refuge is an oasis. Aside from world-class bird watching, there’s hiking, exploring turtle nesting and admiring the vast array of wildflowers and butterflies.

Corona Park

Flushing Meadows Corona Park

The largest park in Queens has something for everyone—green recreational spaces for baseball, soccer and cricket, playgrounds, skateboarding areas, handball courts, dog runs and even the Queens Zoo. The US Open, Citi Field and multiple world's fairs are also held in the beautiful park.

Alley Pond Park

Alley Pond Park

This unique park lies on a ridge of rock and sand and includes nature trails, tulip trees, massive oaks, a bird sanctuary, dog runs, fishing areas, a Picnic Grove, tennis courts and a soccer field.

Forest Park

Forest Park

This park's landscape is rich and lush with high hills, tons of trees, birds galore and breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean and Long Island. Take a stroll through the grounds, check out a live show, ride a horse or get lost on a winding path near the 18-hole golf course.
