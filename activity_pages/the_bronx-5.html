Riverdale

Riverdale

Perched on the hills above the Hudson River are Riverdale’s sprawling properties, boasting incredible views. Parks and gardens line the river, providing ample benches to sit and enjoy sunsets. To the east is the lovely Van Cortlandt Park. It’s prime location coupled with extensive dining options ranging from sushi and Italian to Greek, make Riverdale one of the best neighborhoods in the city.

Fordham

Fordham

In Fordham, you will find Little Italy, Fordham University, and multiple leisure attractions. One of the things that the residents of this location love the most is the community diversity, which is also reflected in the area’s eateries. There are also good Metro and subway links to the surrounding area, so commuting is never a problem.

Castle Hill

Castle Hill

The neighborhood of Castle Hill enjoys a family-friendly, suburban feel. Its southside rocky waterfront offers locals fishing with tranquil views of the Whitestone Bridge. Compared to the rest of the Bronx, Castle hill is quiet and enjoys very little traffic. It has a strong community that is centered around its numerous mom and pop businesses.

City Island

City Island

It's hard to believe that you're within the boundaries of New York City when you're on City Island. It feels like a secluded fishing village, not a neighborhood in one of the biggest cities in the world. If you’re looking for a slower-paced lifestyle, City Island’s small town vibe, great seafood restaurants and quaint shops are just what you've been looking for.
