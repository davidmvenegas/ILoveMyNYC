Staten Island Museum

Staten Island Museum

The Staten Island Museum is located in the borough’s Snug Harbor Cultural Center & Botanical Garden. Dedicated to the heritage of Staten Island, its exhibitions and programs focus on Staten Island’s interrelationship between art, science and history.

National Lighthouse Museum

National Lighthouse Museum

The National Lighthouse Museum educates visitors about the history and technology of the nation’s lighthouses. Visitors receive a brief orientation to the self-guided museum and then are free to explore the fascinating exhibits on lighthouse keepers, foghorns and sound signals, illuminants and optics to name a few.

Museum Of Maritime N&C

Museum Of Maritime Navigation and Communication

With collections of equipment including depth sounders, radar and satellite navigation, this Staten Island museum offers a look at the history and development of maritime communications and wayfinding devices including an exhibit on morse code.

Historic Richmond Town

Historic Richmond Town

Watch history come alive at Historic Richmond Town, a living history village that lets you experience colonial life firsthand. Walk through real restored homes and other buildings including a general store and a courthouse. The museum also has exhibits that portray many aspects of American life from the 1700s through today.
