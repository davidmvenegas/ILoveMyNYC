City Island

City Island

A quaint seaside escape, City Island feels like no other place within the city limits. The Bronx fishing village is full of yacht clubs and waterfront restaurants. It’s popular during the warmer months for its laid back vibe, fresh seafood dining options and range of activities—including scuba diving.

Bronx Walk of Fame

Bronx Walk of Fame

The highest honor a Bronxite can receive is induction to the Bronx Walk of Fame. The Walk of Fame was established to honor those who have made significant accomplishments and contributed to society through their respective fields. Today there are over 120 inductees, with their street signs displayed along the historic Grand Concourse.

Bronx River

Bronx River

One of the marvels of the New York City landscape, the 23-mile Bronx River, winds down through southern Westchester and the Bronx. Sourced from the Kensico Reservoir, it is the only freshwater river in New York City. A corridor of greenery, the river provides a peaceful area for fishing, strolling, biking, boating, and connecting with nature.

Orchard Beach

Orchard Beach

Orchard Beach, the Bronx's only beach, is a very popular hotspot on a hot day. The beach sits on the Long Island Estuary and offers a one-of-a-kind view of City Island. If you want to get out of the sun, you can take a leisurely walk on the boardwalk and stop into some of the many shops and food venues.
