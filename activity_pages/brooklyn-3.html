NYC Transit Museum

NYC Transit Museum

Located in an authentic subway station that was decommissioned in 1936, the New York Transit Museum celebrates the region’s public transportation network and highlights all of its ins and outs—from construction equipment to vintage subway cars to typography in the stations.

Museum of Food & Drink

Museum of Food & Drink

Food is culture. Discover why at the Museum of Food and Drink. MOFAD is a museum that brings the world of food to life with exhibits you can taste, touch, and smell.

Coney Island Museum

Coney Island Museum

On New York’s most famous beach, you’ll find this colorful museum dedicated to America’s playground. Here, the Brooklyn neighborhood’s past is celebrated with fun house mirrors, vintage bumper cars, a 3-D printed model of the original amusement park and many other special exhibits.

Brooklyn Museum

Brooklyn Museum

Brooklyn Museum houses a huge collection categorized by culture, as well as exhibitions from a wide variety of geographic locations and time periods. It’s just beside the Brooklyn Botanic Garden, and allows visitors to easily tour both in the same day.
