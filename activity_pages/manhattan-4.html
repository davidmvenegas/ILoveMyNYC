Union Square Park

Union Square Park

There’s a lot of action in Union Square’s namesake park, where dog lovers and chess players play, activists speak their minds and skateboarders practice their tricks. It’s also the site of the much-loved Union Square Holiday Market and a year-round Greenmarket. If you swing by, you’ll never be bored.

Central Park

Central Park

Central Park is an urban oasis. The 843-acre green space in center Manhattan features rolling meadows and peaceful bodies of water, bringing a sense of calm to the otherwise busy City. The range of activities you can take part in here is nearly unparalleled: hiking, biking, ice-skating, fishing, visiting the Central Park Zoo, seeing a formal English garden and, during the summer months, lounging on a picnic blanket and listening to live concerts.

Bryant Park

Bryant Park

Bryant Park is an urban oasis in the heart of Manhattan that features famous monuments, Le Carousel, concerts, and free summer outdoor movie nights. Winter Village at Bryant Park is the perfect destination during the winter season, with a skating rink at center. The rink offers free admission for ice skating, high-quality rental skates and ice shows from late October to early March.

Washington Square Park

Washington Square Park

Washington Square Park is one of NYC’s most recognizable public spaces. Come feel the energy that makes the place so special - students congregating around fountains, musicians sit on benches strumming guitars, and dogs run gleefully. The park’s black-brick paths and vintage-style streetlights lend it a classic 19th-century feel.
