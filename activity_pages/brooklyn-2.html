Barclays Center

Barclays Center

In the heart of Brooklyn, Barclays Center sets the standard for showcase venues. This state-of-the-art arena is home to the NBA’s Brooklyn Nets, offering intimate seating configurations and unparalleled sightlines for basketball, hockey, musical and numerous other fans.

Grand Army Plaza

Grand Army Plaza

Originally, the Grand Army Plaza was the battlegrounds for the first major battle of the American Revolution. Today, it’s the crossroads where several important roads meet and forms the entrance to Prospect Park, Brooklyn.

Brooklyn Bridge

Brooklyn Bridge

Stretching across the East River, the iconic Brooklyn Bridge connects Lower Manhattan and Brooklyn. One of the most recognizable parts of the New York City skyline, the bridge is a real piece of New York City history. A stroll across the elevated pedestrian walkway provides a true New York City experience.

Coney Island

Coney Island

Known as The Birthplace of the Amusement Park, Coney Island is home to an aquarium, action-packed rides, restaurants, shops, and more. From roller-coasters and go-karts, to live entertainment and boardwalk games - beachfront Coney Island has something for everyone.
